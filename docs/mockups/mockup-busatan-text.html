Intermediate Tunisian dialogue daily life

A short dialogue between two friends ordering coffee in a Tunis café. Focus on everyday Tunisian dialect forms.

قَهْوَة qahwa noun coffee
بِلْحَلِيب bilḥalīb prep+n with milk
بَرَاكَا barāka particle enough
مِنْ min prep of/from
سُكَّر sukkar noun sugar
Trans.I'd like a coffee with milk, no sugar please.
Note. نَحِبّ in Tunisian means "I want / I'd like", unlike MSA where it means "I love". بَرَاكَا مِنْ is a polite refusal formula.

بِلْنِعْنَع bilniʕniʕ prep+n with mint
يَعْطِيكْ الصِّحَّة yaʕṭīk iṣṣiḥḥa phrase bless you
Trans.And I'll have mint tea. Bless you!
Note. يَعْطِيكْ الصِّحَّة — lit. "may God give you health", a polite expression of thanks after receiving service.
Full text
أَشْنُوَّ تَشْرَبْ؟ نَحِبّ قَهْوَة بِلْحَلِيب، بَرَاكَا مِنْ سُكَّر. وَأَنَا نَاخُذْ شَاي بِلْنِعْنَع، يَعْطِيكْ الصِّحَّة.
ašnuwwa tašrab? naḥibb qahwa bilḥalīb, barāka min sukkar. wa-anā nāxuð šāy bilniʕniʕ, yaʕṭīk iṣṣiḥḥa.
What are you drinking? I'd like a coffee with milk, no sugar please. And I'll have mint tea. Bless you!
