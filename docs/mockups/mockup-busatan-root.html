كَتَبَ — k-t-b 3 letters
Core meaning

to write, to inscribe, to record — the fundamental act of putting marks on a surface to communicate meaning

Analysis

The root ك-ت-ب (k-t-b) is one of the most productive roots in Arabic, covering the entire semantic field of writing and recording. The basic form كَتَبَ (kataba, "he wrote") follows the standard Form I triliteral verb pattern.

Notable derived forms: كِتَاب (kitāb, "book") follows the فِعَال pattern for instruments/objects; كَاتِب (kātib, "writer, scribe") is the active participle; مَكْتُوب (maktūb, "written, letter, fate") the passive participle — which acquired the extended meaning of "fate" in many dialects.

In Tunisian dialect: كتب preserves the core meaning. مَكْتُوب is widely used for both "letter" and "fate/destiny".
